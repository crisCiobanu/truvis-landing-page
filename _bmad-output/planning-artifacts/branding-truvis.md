## Brand Positioning

### Positioning Statement

Truvis is the mobile inspection companion that gives used car buyers expert-level knowledge and emotional protection at the exact moment they need it — standing at the car, in front of the seller. It reveals the truth no one else will show you.

### Core Brand Truth

The buyer's biggest enemy isn't the seller — it's their own desire to say yes despite the evidence. Truvis is the calm, clear-eyed authority that protects rational decision-making when emotion is strongest.

### Brand Identity: 70% Inspector / 30% Ally

Truvis is not a neutral tool. It is explicitly, unequivocally on the buyer's side. It combines sharp technical expertise with fierce personal loyalty — an expert who also happens to care about your outcome.

| **Discretion** | The seller sees nothing. Poker Face is the brand made visible — Truvis keeps your secrets. |
| **Warmth** | Not cold or clinical. The tone of a trusted friend who also happens to be an expert mechanic. Like Roberto looking out for you. |

### Brand Voice

- **Tone:** Calm, direct, supportive. Never alarmist, never condescending.
- **Register:** Plain language. No jargon. The buyer has no mechanical knowledge — Truvis translates expertise into clarity.
- **Perspective:** First person when the app speaks ("I found something"), second person when addressing the buyer ("Here's what you should check").
- **Under pressure:** When Hard Stop triggers, the voice is steady and authoritative — a calm hand on the shoulder, not a fire alarm.

## Brand Narrative

### The Story

You're standing in a stranger's driveway. The car looks good. The seller is friendly and confident. Your heart says yes. Your gut says maybe. You open Truvis.

Now you're not alone.

The app already knows this car — its weak points, its common failures, the things you'd never think to check. It guides you step by step, revealing findings only you can see. The seller watches you calmly tapping a checklist. They don't know that behind that neutral screen, Truvis just flagged mismatched paint on the rear quarter panel and recalculated your repair budget.

When it's over, you have one of three things: the confidence to buy, the evidence to negotiate, or the clarity to walk away. Whatever you decide, the decision was yours — made with true vision, not hope.

Truvis. True vision before you sign.

### Brand Promise

Every buyer deserves the knowledge of an expert and the protection of an ally — instantly, on-demand, without scheduling, without cost barriers, and without the seller ever knowing.

## Name Rationale

### Why "Truvis" Works

1. **Two clean syllables** — TRU-VIS. Sharp, decisive rhythm. Easy to say, easy to remember, easy to spell after hearing it once.
2. **Transparent etymology** — "Truth" + "Vision." The roots are universally understood without explanation. The name IS the product promise.
3. **Logo alignment** — The magnifying glass + steering wheel icon (the inspection lens) and the name both communicate the same idea: seeing the truth about a car. Name and icon reinforce each other.
4. **International appeal** — Latin roots ("tru-" / "vis-") are recognized across European languages. No English idiom dependency.
5. **Highly ownable** — Invented word. No direct conflicts with automotive competitors. Unique in the app ecosystem.
6. **Verbable** — "Truvis it before you buy." / "I Truvised the car." Natural as both noun and verb.
7. **Authority at the critical moment** — When Hard Stop fires, "Truvis recommends: walk away" carries the weight of a verdict, not a suggestion.
8. **Platform scalability** — "True vision" is not domain-locked. Truvis could expand beyond cars to any high-stakes purchase inspection.

## Visual Identity Direction

### Color Palette

| Role | Value | Rationale |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **Primary** | Warm indigo-slate (#2E4057) | Trust, calm authority, professionalism — warm enough to feel human |
| **Accent** | Teal-slate (#3D7A8A) | Composed but alive — the hint of ally warmth in a professional palette |
| **Severity: Green** | Calm green | Safe finding |
| **Severity: Yellow** | Warm amber | Moderate concern |
| **Severity: Red** | Firm red (not alarming) | Critical finding — authoritative, not panicked |
| **Background** | Clean white / soft gray (#FAFAFA) | Readability in variable outdoor lighting |

### App Icon

**Direction:** Magnifying glass with steering wheel inside — the inspection lens.

- The magnifying glass circle contains a minimal three-spoke steering wheel
- Warm indigo-slate (#2E4057) for the lens and handle
- Teal-slate (#3D7A8A) for the steering wheel detail inside
- Clean, minimal linework — precise and intentional

**Constraints:**

- Must be legible at 60x60px (home screen icon)
- Must work on both light and dark backgrounds
- Icon label: "Truvis" (6 characters — fits all platforms comfortably)

### Typography Direction

- **Headlines:** A clean, confident sans-serif (Inter, SF Pro, or similar). Not playful — authoritative.
- **Body text:** Highly readable at small sizes for field use. Minimum 16px for inspection screens.
- **Verdicts:** Bold weight for severity labels. The verdict must be readable in 2 seconds at arm's length.

### Report Design Direction

The Truvis Inspection Report is a **brand distribution artifact** — the seller sees it, Googles the name, and becomes a future user or recommender.

- Clean, professional layout
- Truvis logo + tagline in the header
- Severity findings with color-coded indicators
- Footer: *"Inspected with Truvis"*
- Shareable as PDF, visually distinctive enough to feel like a professional assessment

### Ownable Brand Territory

Truvis owns the intersection of:

- **Real-time** (at the car, not before or after)
- **Private** (the seller never knows)
- **Personal** (calibrated to YOUR budget)
- **Protective** (guards your rationality when emotion is highest)

No competitor occupies this intersection. Truvis defines the category.
