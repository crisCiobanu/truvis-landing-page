# Product Brief: Truvis Landing Page

## Executive Summary

Truvis is a mobile app that puts an expert car inspector in every used-car buyer's pocket — with severity scoring, model-specific intelligence, and behavioral guardrails that protect buyers from overpaying or walking into a bad deal. The app launches into a $2.31 trillion global used car market where zero consumer-facing guided inspection tools exist.

The **Truvis landing page** is the app's digital front door: a conversion-optimized web presence that evolves through two phases. Pre-launch, it builds brand credibility, captures a waitlist of high-intent buyers, and seeds organic discovery through SEO-optimized content and multi-channel traffic acquisition. Post-launch, it becomes the primary funnel driving app store downloads, reinforced by real user stories and inspection success data.

This isn't a static brochure. It's a living acquisition engine — part brand story, part content hub, part conversion machine.

## The Problem

Used-car buyers face one of the highest-stakes consumer decisions with almost no trusted guidance. Professional inspections cost €100-300 and require scheduling days in advance. Only 2% of buyers trust dealerships (Q4 2025 consumer survey). Over 40% experience anxiety during the purchase process. And buyers routinely lose hundreds of euros to hidden issues they didn't catch.

These buyers are already searching for help. "How to inspect a used car" is a high-intent query where no consumer app owns the conversation. They find generic checklists, professional services they can't afford, or OBD scanner apps that miss everything you can see with your eyes. Nobody is meeting them where they are — on their phone, at the moment of decision, with guidance that actually protects them.

Truvis solves this. But first, people need to know it exists. The landing page closes that gap: it captures demand that's already searching, builds trust before the app is even available, and converts visitors into users the moment it launches.

## The Solution

A dual-phase web presence with four core functions:

**1. Brand & Credibility Engine**
A landing page that communicates Truvis's unique value in under 3 seconds. The hero leads with the financial angle — *the money buyers lose by inspecting blind* — then walks visitors through the problem they already feel, introduces the six capabilities no other tool offers (Severity Calibrator, Model DNA, Personal Risk Calibration, Poker Face Mode, Hard Stop Protocol, Negotiation Report), and builds trust through social proof and transparent positioning.

**2. Waitlist & Content Hook (Pre-Launch)**
Email capture paired with an immediate value exchange — a free Used Car Buyer's Guide (covering what to check, red flags to spot, negotiation tactics). This gives visitors something useful today while building a launch-day audience. The content hook reinforces Truvis's authority and keeps the brand top-of-mind until the app ships.

**3. SEO Blog & Organic Acquisition Channel**
A content hub targeting high-intent search queries: "how to inspect a used car," "used car red flags," "how to negotiate a used car price." Each article funnels readers toward the waitlist (pre-launch) or app store (post-launch). This transforms the landing page from a one-time visit into a compounding organic growth engine.

**4. Multi-Channel Traffic Acquisition**
Organic SEO alone won't fill the funnel for a new domain. The landing page is supported by a diversified traffic strategy:
- **Social media content** — Short-form video on TikTok and Instagram Reels: car inspection tips, red flag spotting, "would you buy this car?" engagement hooks. Content repurposed from blog articles and app capabilities.
- **Influencer partnerships** — Collaborate with automotive content creators and used-car review channels to demonstrate Truvis's value through authentic inspection walkthroughs.
- **Paid acquisition** — Google Ads targeting high-intent keywords ("used car inspection app," "check used car before buying"), Play Store/App Store search ads (post-launch), and targeted social ads driving to waitlist (pre-launch) or app store (post-launch).

### Post-Launch Evolution

After launch, the page swaps the waitlist for app store download buttons and layers in:
- **Real user stories** — "How Elena avoided a €3,000 repair" / "How Marco saved €700 on his Seat León"
- **Inspection statistics** — live counters of inspections completed, money saved, bad deals avoided
- **App store ratings and testimonials** — social proof from actual users

## What Makes This Different

The landing page inherits Truvis's core differentiation: **there is no competitor to compare against.** No consumer app combines guided inspection + severity scoring + behavioral protection + negotiation reports. The landing page doesn't need to win a comparison — it needs to define a new category. The world's first guided mobile inspection companion for used-car buyers.

The content strategy amplifies this. While competitors (Carfax, Lemon Squad) target buyers who already know what they want, Truvis's SEO and social content captures buyers at the earliest moment of uncertainty — when they're Googling "what to look for in a used car" or scrolling TikTok for car-buying tips. The landing page meets them with authority, earns trust through free value, and converts them before they ever encounter an alternative.

The brand voice carries through: 70% Inspector, 30% Ally. The same cheeky co-pilot personality from the app — steady authority when explaining risks, conspiratorial warmth when it's just you and the app. This isn't corporate SaaS marketing. It's a trusted friend who happens to know everything about cars.

## Who This Serves

**Primary: Used-car buyers actively searching for guidance**
- **Marco** (25-35, first-time buyer) — anxious, Googling "what to check when buying a used car," needs confidence
- **Elena** (30-45, burned before) — searching for tools to protect herself, needs proof this won't happen again

**Secondary: Referral & social traffic**
- Friends/family sent a Truvis inspection report link and want to learn more about the app
- TikTok/Reels viewers who discover Truvis through short-form content
- Readers who find blog content organically and convert over multiple visits

**Geographic scope:** English-first launch. The page architecture must support internationalisation (i18n-ready) so French and German can be added in a subsequent phase, targeting the EU's largest private-sale markets (France ~5.5M, Germany ~7M annual transactions).

## Success Criteria

### Pre-Launch
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Waitlist signups | 1,000+ before launch day |
| Email capture rate | >3% of unique visitors |
| Blog organic traffic | 500+ monthly organic sessions within 3 months of first publish |
| Content hook engagement | >10% of waitlist signups download the free guide |

### Post-Launch
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| App store click-through rate | >3% of landing page visitors |
| Blog → download conversion | >1% of blog readers click through to app store |
| User story engagement | Stories are most-read blog category within 6 months |
| Organic search traffic | 30%+ of total landing page traffic within 6 months |

## Scope

### In Scope (V1 — Pre-Launch)
- Single-page landing site with hero, problem, features, social proof placeholder, blog section, FAQ, footer CTA
- Email waitlist capture with content hook delivery (automated)
- Blog infrastructure supporting SEO-optimized articles
- Responsive design (mobile-first)
- i18n-ready architecture (English only at launch, structured for FR/DE addition later)
- Analytics integration (visitor tracking, conversion events, UTM support)
- GDPR compliance: cookie consent banner, explicit email opt-in, privacy policy, data processing documentation compliant with EU regulations
- Social media link integration (TikTok, Instagram, etc.)

### In Scope (V1.1 — Post-Launch Update)
- App store buttons replacing waitlist CTA
- User stories section with real testimonials
- Live inspection statistics integration
- App store rating/review showcase

### In Scope (V1.2 — Internationalisation)
- French and German language support for landing page and blog
- Localised SEO content strategy for FR/DE markets

### Out of Scope
- User accounts or login functionality on the web
- In-browser inspection tools or demos
- Payment processing or subscription management
- Community forum or user-generated content
- B2B / "For Professionals" features
- Native mobile landing page (the app IS the mobile experience)

## Vision

If the landing page succeeds, it becomes more than a download funnel. Within 12 months, it's the go-to organic destination for used-car buying guidance — the place buyers find before they even know they need an app. The blog and social content build a compounding moat, driving acquisition costs toward zero for organic visitors.

The TikTok and social media presence creates a flywheel: short-form content drives landing page traffic, blog content builds SEO authority, the waitlist builds launch-day momentum, and post-launch user stories feed back into social content. Each channel reinforces the others.

At 18+ months, the content hub evolves alongside the app's platform ambitions and expands into French and German markets, establishing Truvis as the European standard for smart used-car buying.
